Audiobook Cover Design

Created with GIMP for LibriVox.org, a volunteer-run effort to provide free, public domain audiobooks in many languages. As required, each cover was created using public domain or Creative Commons 0 resources and open fonts, as well as completed within a 2-week timeframe.

With these covers, my goal is to provide a visualizer for the story, grab the listener's attention, and increase usability by making the title readable at thumbnail size. I also love to challenge myself by volunteering for a variety of book genres and experimenting with different artistic styles.

Experience

Oct 2019–Jun 2022

Served as a full-stack developer for Covenant Eyes (Owosso, MI), a SaaS company empowering people to tackle their addiction to explicit content online. My work supported Covenant Eyes' Screen Accountability™ service in a variety of ways, including:

  • Developing internal services and customer-facing applications
  • Creating and presenting training material on my work in telemetry
  • Helping product designers assess feasibility of designs and ideate new options

In winter-spring 2021, I reworked large portions of the customer self-service portal (My Account) to implement a new brand look-and-feel. Closely guided by the UX team, my efforts included building out new UI component library elements, creating new layouts in Vue.js, and making extensive changes to the CSS stylesheets. The resulting high-fidelity implementation evokes the Covenant Eyes brand, improves usability for our members, and is more maintainable for developers. See examples here in the support documentation for My Account.

Nov 2018–Oct 2019

Led the software team for South Sound 911 (Tacoma, WA), providing applications and support for 41 police and fire agencies across Pierce County, WA. In addition to managing six developers, I advocated for UX processes on the tech team, assisted project managers (including taking on a PM role for smaller projects), and was Tier 3 support for mission-critical services.

Oct 2014–Oct 2018

Worked as a full-stack developer for South Sound 911 (Tacoma, WA). Collaboratively built software used by local law enforcement, firefighters, dispatchers, and records management personnel. Educated team members on design best practices and conducted usability tests.
